Fort-de-France is the gateway for Martinique cruise excursions. This practical guide covers where ships dock, how to choose a shore excursion, what to expect ashore, and how to make the most of a typical 5–7 hour port day in this distinctive French Caribbean destination.

Where Ships Dock

Fort-de-France Cruise Port

Most vessels berth at Pointe Simon, a downtown pier within walking distance of La Savane park, shops and the covered market. Larger ships may anchor in the bay and tender passengers to La Tourelles, slightly south of the city centre.

Both locations connect easily to taxis, tour pickups and local buses. Confirm your pier assignment on the ship's daily programme — it affects where you meet your Martinique shore excursion guide.

  • ✔ Pointe Simon: walkable to downtown Fort-de-France
  • ✔ La Tourelles: tender port, short taxi to centre
  • ✔ Euro currency — ATMs near the waterfront

What Cruise Passengers Can See in One Day

A well-planned Martinique cruise excursion covers either northern highlights (Pelée, Saint-Pierre, optional rum stop) or southern beaches and coastal scenery — not both. If your tour returns early, explore Fort-de-France's Schoelcher Library, the market on Rue Isambert, or a café along the waterfront.

Practical Port Tips

Language

French is official. Tour guides on Martinique cruise excursions typically speak English. A few French phrases are appreciated at markets and cafés.

Timing

Allow 60–90 minutes before all aboard. North routes need longer drives; book early departures on short port days.

Independent Exploration

Downtown Fort-de-France is walkable from Pointe Simon. Distillery visits, Saint-Pierre and southern beaches require organised transport.

Port Guide FAQ

Where do cruise ships dock in Martinique?›

Pointe Simon in downtown Fort-de-France or La Tourelles via tender. Check your ship's daily programme for the exact pier.

Is Martinique French-speaking?›

Yes. French is the official language. English is common on organised Martinique shore excursions and in tourist areas around the cruise port.

Is Martinique good for cruise passengers?›

Martinique offers a richer port experience than many Caribbean stops — French culture, volcanoes, rum and beaches within reach of Fort-de-France on a single shore excursion.
