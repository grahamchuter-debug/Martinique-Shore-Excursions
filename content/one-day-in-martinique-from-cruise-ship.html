North vs South: Pick One

Martinique is larger than it looks on a map. Covering both Mount Pelée and southern beaches in one port day is not realistic. North suits culture, volcanoes and rum; south suits beaches and relaxed coastal scenery.

Read the full comparison on our excursions hub or the Fort-de-France port guide.

Fort-de-France Without a Full Tour

If your ship arrives late or you prefer a lighter day, explore downtown Fort-de-France on foot from Pointe Simon:

  • ✔ La Savane — central park with statue of Empress Joséphine
  • ✔ Schoelcher Library — ornate iron-built landmark
  • ✔ Grand Marché — spices, produce and local crafts
  • ✔ Waterfront cafés — French pastries and Creole dishes

For Saint-Pierre, distilleries or southern beaches, book a Martinique cruise excursion — public transport is limited for tight ship schedules.

One-Day Planning FAQ

How much time do I need in Martinique port?›

Most ships allow 5–9 hours ashore. A 4–5 hour north or south excursion uses the bulk of that time comfortably, with buffer before all aboard.

Should I choose north or south Martinique?›

North for Pelée, Saint-Pierre and rum. South for beaches and Diamond Rock. One port day, one direction.

Can you visit a rum distillery on a cruise stop?›

Yes — north Martinique tours often include a distillery visit. See our rum guide for what to expect.
