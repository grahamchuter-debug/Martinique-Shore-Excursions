Cruise-Friendly Design

Why This Tour Works for Cruise Passengers

South Martinique is closer to Fort-de-France than the northern volcano route, which means more time at beaches and viewpoints and a comfortable margin before all aboard. The pace is relaxed — ideal when you want French Caribbean sunshine without long winding drives.

Les Anses-d'Arlet's trio of coves offers some of Martinique's prettiest swim spots. Diamond Rock — a striking islet off the Diamant coast — provides iconic photo opportunities. Coastal villages add local colour without demanding strenuous activity.

  • ✔ Shorter transfer times from port
  • ✔ Beach breaks built into the itinerary
  • ✔ Great for families and casual travellers

Practical Information

Pickup: Fort-de-France cruise port — confirm pier (Pointe Simon or tender) when booking.

What to bring: Swimwear, towel, reef-safe sunscreen, water shoes, euros for beach cafés.

Driving: Roughly 30–45 minutes to Les Anses-d'Arlet from the port — less than north routes.

Alternatives: For volcanoes and Saint-Pierre, see the north Martinique tour.

South Tour FAQ

Are south Martinique tours good for families?›

Yes. Beach stops, easy walking and shorter drives make south tours among the most family-friendly Martinique shore excursions available from Fort-de-France.

Should I choose south over north Martinique?›

Choose south if beaches and swimming are your priority. Choose north for Mount Pelée, Saint-Pierre and rum distilleries. With one port day, most passengers pick one direction.

Do south tours return to the ship on time?›

Shorter distances give south tours excellent return-to-ship reliability. Reputable operators still track schedules and plan buffer time before all aboard.

Can I swim on a south Martinique excursion?›

Most south tours include a beach or snorkel stop at Les Anses-d'Arlet or nearby coves. Bring swimwear and confirm water activities when booking.
