Martinique produces some of the world's finest rhum agricole — rum distilled from fresh sugarcane juice rather than molasses. For cruise passengers, a distillery visit is one of the most authentic Martinique shore excursion experiences, typically included on north island tours from Fort-de-France.

Rhum Agricole

Martinique's Rum Heritage

Martinique's rhum agricole carries a French AOC designation — the only rum in the world with appellation d'origine contrôlée status. The distinction matters: agricole rum captures the fresh, grassy character of sugarcane harvested and pressed on the same day.

Distilleries cluster in northern Martinique, making them a natural fit for shore excursions that also visit Mount Pelée and Saint-Pierre. A typical visit lasts 30–45 minutes and includes a guided walk through the production area and a tasting.

Notable Distilleries Near Cruise Routes

Depaz Distillery

At the foot of Mount Pelée with estate grounds and a polished visitor experience. Frequently included on north Martinique tours.

J.M. Distillery

In the lush Fonds-Préville area. Known for premium vieux rhums and a scenic approach through rainforest.

Neisson Distillery

A family-run operation in Le Carbet popular with rum enthusiasts seeking artisanal production.

Habitation Clément

In the south near Le François — possible on extended tours but less common on standard cruise excursions.

What Cruise Passengers Should Know

  • ✔Book a north tour — distillery stops are standard on north Martinique shore excursions, not south beach routes.
  • ✔Tastings are optional — you can observe production without sampling. Age restrictions may apply.
  • ✔Buying bottles — sealed rum can be packed in checked luggage subject to your cruise line's alcohol policy. Carry-on limits apply for flights home.
  • ✔Timing — distillery visits fit within a 4–5 hour excursion alongside Saint-Pierre and Pelée viewpoints.

Rum Distillery FAQ

Can you visit a rum distillery on a cruise stop?›

Yes. North Martinique shore excursions from Fort-de-France commonly include Depaz, J.M. or similar distilleries. Confirm the stop when booking your tour.

What is rhum agricole?›

Rhum agricole is made from fresh-pressed sugarcane juice, not molasses. Martinique's version has AOC protected status and a distinctly grassy, terroir-driven flavour profile.

How long does a distillery visit take?›

Plan on 30–45 minutes including a guided tour and optional tasting. This fits comfortably within a north Martinique cruise excursion itinerary.
