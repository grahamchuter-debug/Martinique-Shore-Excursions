Why Choose Martinique
Shore Excursions

Martinique stands apart from many Caribbean cruise ports. As an overseas region of France, it blends Parisian café culture with Creole heritage, volcanic drama and lush rainforest. Cruise passengers docking at Fort-de-France discover a destination where baguettes sit beside boudin créole, and Mount Pelée looms over a coastline dotted with rum distilleries and turquoise coves.

With typically 5–7 hours ashore, Martinique shore excursions focus on realistic north or south routes — volcano viewpoints and Saint-Pierre history in the north, beaches and coastal villages in the south — all planned around your ship's all aboard time.

Fort-de-France Cruise Port

Port Tips for
Cruise Passengers

Ships dock at Fort-de-France's Pointe Simon pier or anchor offshore with tender service to La Tourelles. Downtown Fort-de-France — La Savane park, Schoelcher Library and the covered market — is walkable from Pointe Simon when you have time before or after a tour.

  • ✔ Confirm your pier assignment on the ship's daily programme
  • ✔ Book north or south tours early on multi-ship days
  • ✔ Carry euros for market stalls and small purchases
  • ✔ Allow 60–90 minutes buffer before all aboard

Cruise FAQ

Martinique Shore Excursion Questions

Is Martinique good for cruise passengers?›

Yes. Martinique rewards cruise passengers who want more than a beach break. French Caribbean culture, volcanic scenery, rum heritage and compact north or south routes make it one of the more distinctive Lesser Antilles port calls — especially if you book a well-timed shore excursion from Fort-de-France.

Where do cruise ships dock in Martinique?›

Most ships use Pointe Simon in downtown Fort-de-France. Some vessels anchor in the bay and tender passengers to La Tourelles. Both are close to taxis, tour pickups and the city centre. Check your ship's pier assignment the morning of arrival.

Can you visit Mount Pelée from a cruise ship?›

Yes, on a north Martinique shore excursion. Tours typically reach viewpoints and interpretive stops rather than summit hikes, which need a full day. Expect roughly 45–60 minutes driving from Fort-de-France to the Pelée area, with Saint-Pierre often included on the same route.

Is Saint-Pierre worth visiting?›

Absolutely. Saint-Pierre — known as the Pompeii of the Caribbean — was destroyed by Mount Pelée's 1902 eruption. Today its museums, ruins and waterfront tell a powerful story. It is a highlight of most north Martinique cruise excursions. See our Saint-Pierre guide.

Are Martinique shore excursions suitable for families?›

Yes. South Martinique tours with beach stops work well for families with children. North tours suit curious older kids and teens interested in volcanoes and history. Look for small-group operators with clear duration and walking-level descriptions before booking.

Do Martinique tours return to the ship on time?›

Reputable Martinique shore excursion operators track ship schedules and plan routes with buffer time. Always confirm your all aboard time at booking and choose tours designed for cruise port days rather than open-ended sightseeing.

Should I choose north or south Martinique?›

Choose north for Mount Pelée, Saint-Pierre and rainforest scenery. Choose south for beaches, Diamond Rock views and a more relaxed pace. With one port day, most passengers pick one direction. Our excursions hub compares both in detail.

Is Martinique French-speaking?›

Yes. French is the official language and Creole is widely spoken. Tour guides on Martinique cruise excursions typically speak English, and tourist areas around Fort-de-France accommodate international visitors. A few French phrases are appreciated but not essential on organised tours.

Can you visit a rum distillery on a cruise stop?›

Yes. Northern Martinique tours often include a rhum agricole distillery stop such as Depaz or J.M. Visits are usually 30–45 minutes — enough for a guided tour and tasting. Read our rum distillery guide for details.
